# Monty Python’s Cocurricular Mediaeval Reenactment Programme Rules Primer 

### Core Rules

1. Choose your course of action
2. Determine which one of your **Traits** is being called upon
3. Roll the die that corresponds to that **Trait**.
4. Apply any pluses or minuses from **Accoutrements** or **Retainers**.
5. Announce the resulte to the HoLE

### Situations (Character Classes)

There are 10 situations. Each has: 

- A social class designation (Upper, middle, or lower, which influences how NPCs may react to you).
- Spiffing Serious Ability (Special Skills, Feature)
- 3 to 5 **Traits** (Skills/Saving Throws, more on these in the Traits section)

  _Note:_ Situations have both pre-selected traits and traits you can choose from.

- An **Accoutrement** for each **Trait** (gear that provides a modifier to one or more skills, can provide a constant effect, and/or can have a one-time-use effect)
- 1 **Trait** they are Indifferent to (may not use nor be affected by). 

  _Note:_ If your character has a **Retainer** (follower who carries 2 accoutrements) or 2, that takes that many **Trait** specialization spots, hence the 3 to 5. Some situations have **Retainers** by default. Others have the choice between more **Traits** or **Retainers**.

### Traits (Skills/Saving Throws)

There are 18 **Traits**. Your level in a **Trait** determines the dice you roll for it. If a **Trait** is not on your character sheet, you can still use it, you will just roll a d4 for it. 

_**Spam:**_ If you ever **Spam** (roll a 1), you get a **Demerit** and go down a level in the **Trait** (if it’s on your character sheet). 

_**Strewth:**_ If you ever **Strewth** (roll the maximum on the die), your level in the **Trait** goes up (if it’s on your character sheet), and you roll the new die (or another d4 if it’s not on your character sheet) and add the results.

### Merits, Demerits, and Beshrewments

The GM plays the Head of Light Entertainment (HoLE) with one of 18 personalities determined randomly at the start of the session. Each one has different likes and dislikes, number of **Demerits** to earn a **Beshrewment**, and a **Beshrewment** table.

#### Merits

These are points you can use to grant yourself advantage on a roll. Or to cancel out a Demerit you have or are about to receive.

You get Merits by

- Pleasing the HoLE
- The GM awards you one (these are not the same)

#### Demerits

These points do nothing on their own. But if you ever earn the number of **Demerits** that equals or exceeds the HoLE threshold, you turn in the **Demerits** and trigger a **Beshrewment**.

And only God can help you then. If he's around.

You get **Demerits** by

- Taking a Roll too seriously (if your final result is above 20. Above 40 you get 2 Demerits)
- **Spamming:** Rolling a 1
- Offending the HoLE
- Offending the GM (these are not the same)
