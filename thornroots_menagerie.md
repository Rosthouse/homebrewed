# Thornroot’s Folly: A Menagerie of Chaos
*A Dungeons & Dragons Oneshot Adventure*

When the guild sends you to retrieve a few missing dwarves from a reclusive Treant collector’s menagerie, you expect an easy job. But Thornroot’s "harmless" collection hides a deadly secret: his prize specimen, the Uiik—a deceptively adorable predator—has escaped, and the dwarves aren’t as innocent as they seem. Trapped inside by an unbreakable emergency ward, you must outwit a creature that mimics your voice, outmaneuver cunning dwarves with their own schemes, and survive a menagerie where every shadow could be your last. Will you restore order, or become just another exhibit in Thornroot’s growing collection?

## 1. Review the Characters
### Players
- **Role:** Adventurers hired by the guild to retrieve the Ironvein Clan dwarves from Thornroot’s menagerie.
- **Hook:** The guild offers a reward for the safe return of the dwarves, who were sold to Thornroot as part of a debt settlement.

### Key NPCs
- **Thornroot:** A naive but charming Treant collector who runs the menagerie.
- **Ironvein Clan Dwarves (Grimli, Thorgar, Brinja, Durnik):** Greedy captives plotting to take over the menagerie and turn it into a zoo.
- **The Uiik:** A deceptively cute, six-legged predator that mimics sounds and drains its prey.


## 2. Create a Strong Start
### The Guild’s Task
- The players are hired to retrieve the dwarves from Thornroot’s menagerie.
- They receive:
  - A **leaf-shaped amulet** (proof of safe passage; glows near creatures Thornroot wants).
  - A **crude map** of the facility.
  - A **dwarven clan token** (to verify the dwarves’ identities).
- **Hook:** *"Thornroot’s just a weird old tree who loves creatures. How dangerous could it be?"*


\column

**Atmosphere:**
  - The menagerie is inside a **massive, hollow tree** with a **150-foot-tall inner tree**.
  - The air smells like a **forest after rain**, and the enclosures are built into the **old roots of the outer tree**.
  - Light filters in from **natural and magical sources**.

## 3. Outline Potential Scenes

### Scene 1: The Tour
- Thornroot **proudly shows off his menagerie**, introducing each creature with enthusiasm.
- **Clues:**
  - The **Uiik mimics sounds** (player voices, creature noises).
  - The **dwarves watch the players closely**, sizing them up.
  - **Dwarven runes** are visible on some of the wards (Investigation DC 15).

- **Thornroot’s Dialogue:**
  - *"Ah, my beauty, don’t mind the guests!"* (to the griffon).
  - *"Oh, she’s a sweet thing. Just needs a little supervision."* (about the Uiik).

### Scene 2: The Uiik’s Escape
- The Uiik **suddenly appears in the owlbear’s enclosure**, drains it, and **escapes into the ventilation shafts**.
- The **emergency security system activates**, trapping everyone inside the menagerie.
- **Thornroot panics:** *"The wards—someone *tampered* with them!"*

- **Clues:**
  - The **owlbear’s bones collapse** as the Uiik drains it.
  - The **ventilation shafts rattle** as the Uiik moves through them.

\page
{{pageNumber,auto}}

### Scene 3: The Chase
- The Uiik **stalks the players** through the menagerie, using the **ventilation shafts** to ambush them.
- **Tactics:**
  - It **drops from above** onto a player’s shoulders (Dexterity save DC 14 to avoid).
  - It **mimics the players’ voices** to unnerve them.
  - It **retreat into the shafts** if injured, only to reappear later.

- **Player Options:**
  - **Set traps** (e.g., collapse a section of the shaft).
  - **Use the shafts** to scout or ambush the Uiik (Small/Tiny creatures only).
  - **Lure it into a dead end** (e.g., the control room).

### Scene 4: The Control Room
- Thornroot **fumbles with the warding stones**, realizing they’ve been sabotaged.
- The **emergency security orb** (red and pulsating) **traps everyone inside**.
- **Clues:**
  - The **dwarves’ orb is the only one still glowing**.
  - **Scratches on the connections** reveal dwarven tampering (Investigation DC 16).
  - Thornroot **mumbles:** *"The magic’s still here… so why aren’t they working?"*

- **Player Options:**
  - **Attempt to reactivate an orb** (Arcana DC 18; temporary fix).
  - **Confront Thornroot** about the dwarves.

### Scene 5: The Dwarves’ Betrayal
- The players reach the **dwarves’ hold**, where the dwarves **taunt them** while holding Grimli’s token.
- **Dwarven Dialogue:**
  - Grimli: *"You’ll never get this."* (while clutching the token)
  - Thorgar: *"We’re not *prisoners*. We’re *businessmen*."*
- **Player Options:**
  - **Negotiate/Fight/Steal the token** to destroy it.
  - **Threaten to feed them to the Uiik** (they’ll panic and hand it over).

- **Token Destroyed:**
  - The **wards reset**, and the Uiik is **pulled back into its enclosure**.
  - Thornroot **sighs:** *"It’s not even that strong. A box would’ve held it."*

\column

### Scene 6: The Final Resolution
- **Uiik:** Trapped in its enclosure, **snarling at the players**.
- **Dwarves:**
  - If **freed**, they **escape to start their own zoo**.
  - If **left with Thornroot**, they **plot revenge** or **try to sabotage him again**.
- **Thornroot:**
  - If **alive**, he **offers the players a reward** (e.g., a magic item or a favor).
  - If **dead**, his **last words** are: *"Protect… my collection…"*
- **Emergency Ward:** **Deactivates** once the original wards are restored.

## 4. Define Secrets and Clues

### Secrets About the Uiik
1. It **mimics the last sounds it hears** (player voices, creature noises).
2. It **drains prey completely**, leaving hollow bones and sagging skin.
3. It’s **not strong**—just **relentless**. A locked box or reinforced cage would hold it.
4. It **can squeeze through any gap wider than its skull**.
5. It **uses the ventilation shafts** to move unseen.

### Secrets About the Dwarves
6. They **let the Uiik out** to cause chaos and take over the menagerie.
7. Their enclosure’s wards are the **only ones untouched**—because they reinforced them.
8. They have a **ward-piercing dagger** (3 charges, recharges 1d4/day at the forge).
9. They were **sold to Thornroot** by rivals and see the menagerie as a **business opportunity**.
10. They **tampered with the wards nightly** while Thornroot slept.

### Secrets About Thornroot
11. He’s **not evil**—just **obsessed** and **naïve**.
12. He **knew the Uiik was dangerous** but trusted his wards.
13. He’s been **tricked before**—this isn’t his first betrayal.
14. He **cannot disable the emergency security system**—it’s designed to be unbreakable.

### Secrets About the Facility
15. The **emergency system activates** when a ward fails, **trapping everyone inside**.
16. The **vents are big enough for the Uiik** but not for most other creatures.
17. The **mimic is terrified of the Uiik** and may help the players.
18. The **griffon hates the Uiik** and will attack it on sight.
19. The **zombies’ wards are tied to the Uiik’s enclosure**—if it fails, they’re free to roam.

## 5. Develop Fantastic Locations

### Grand Menagerie
- **Description:** A **colossal hollow tree** with a **150-foot-tall inner tree**. Enclosures are built into the **old root alcoves**.
- **Atmosphere:**
  - **Smell:** Forest after rain, damp earth, and dwarven ale.
  - **Sound:** Creature calls, dwarven hammering, and the Uiik’s faint squeaks.
  - **Light:** Natural sunlight and **magical orbs** (warding stones).
- **Key Features:**
  - **Thornroot’s workspace** (desk, ledger, scrying orb).
  - **Elevated walkways** connecting the enclosures.

### Uiik’s Enclosure
- **Description:** A **plain forest scene**—bushes, small trees, and a large rock. So unremarkable it barely registers.
- **Clues:**
  - **Scratch marks** on the glass (from the Uiik’s claws).
  - **Fur and leather scraps** (remnants of past meals).
  - **Stream with a coppery scent** (old blood).

### Dwarves’ Hold
- **Description:** A **cramped but well-equipped** underground space with a **forge, smithy, and brewery**.
- **Clues:**
  - **Ward-piercing dagger** hidden in the forge.
  - **Dwarven tools** near the vents.
  - **Fresh scratches** on the ward connections.

\column

### Control Room
- **Description:** An **elevated alcove** overlooking the menagerie, with **five warding stones** and a **red, pulsating emergency orb**.
- **Key Features:**
  - **Warding Stones:** One glowing (dwarves’ enclosure), others dark but intact.
  - **Emergency Orb:** Cannot be disabled; **traps everyone inside** until the original wards are restored.
  - **Scrying Orb:** Grants one use of *Scrying* for magic users.

---
### Ventilation Shafts
- **Description:** A **labyrinth of hollowed root channels** and **ancient waterways**, now repurposed for ventilation.
- **Key Features:**
  - **Narrow and twisting** (only the Uiik, mimic, and zombies can fit).
  - **Glowing lichen** provides dim light.
  - **Scratch marks and fur tufts** from the Uiik’s passage.

## Appendix A: Monster Stat Blocks

### Uiik

At first glance, a Uiik appears to be nothing more than an adorable, fluffy creature—its calico fur, large kitten-like eyes, and tiny frame evoke an overwhelming urge to cuddle it. But those who succumb to its charm soon learn the truth: the Uiik is a nightmare wrapped in innocence. Its wide, toothy maw, capable of unhinging to reveal rows of needle-like teeth, betrays its true nature as a relentless predator.
Uiiks are solitary hunters, stalking their prey with terrifying patience. They mimic the calls of young animals to lure the unsuspecting into their grasp, only to strike with a bone-crushing pounce. Once locked onto a target, a Uiik will not let go, draining the very marrow from its victim’s bones. No one has ever tamed a Uiik, and those who hunt them often find themselves the hunted.

**A Persistence Hunter.** Uiiks are built for endurance. They can go days without food, tracking their chosen prey with single-minded focus. Their eggs hatch without a mother’s presence, and the first hatchling to emerge will devour its siblings, ensuring only the strongest survive. This ruthless efficiency extends to their hunting style—once a Uiik locks onto a target, escape is nearly impossible.

**A Deceptive Killer.** Despite their tiny size, Uiiks are agile and deadly over short distances. Their ability to blend into their surroundings, combined with their innocent appearance, makes them one of the most dangerous creatures a traveler might encounter. Adventurers who underestimate a Uiik’s cuteness do so at their own peril.

## Appendix B: Magic Items

#### Mixtape of Sadness
*Wondrous item, Legendary (requires attunement)*
:
This knob is pretty nice. When attached to a door, it allows a user to
open that door with the strength of the nearest animal. For example, if
there is a cow nearby, the user will have the "strength of a cow" while
opening this door.

#### Ward-Piercing Dagger
*Weapon (dagger), uncommon (requires attunement by a dwarf or someone proficient with smith’s tools)*
:
This ornate dagger is etched with dwarven runes and glows faintly blue when near a magical barrier. As an action, you can use the dagger to cut through a magical ward or barrier as if it were nonmagical. This effect lasts for 1 minute or until you deactivate it (no action required).

The dagger has 3 charges and regains 1d4 expended charges at dawn if placed in a dwarven forge for at least 1 hour. If the dagger’s charges are depleted, it functions as a nonmagical dagger until recharged.

#### Grimli’s Token
*Wondrous item, rare*
:
This ornate pendant is etched with dwarven clan runes and glows faintly red when near other dwarven magic. If the token is targeted by the *Dispel Magic* spell (DC 17), all dwarven tampering on the menagerie’s wards is undone, restoring the original wards and deactivating the emergency security system.

While holding the token, dwarves of the Ironvein Clan treat you as their leader, obeying simple commands, though they may still scheme behind your back.


#### Thornroot’s Scrying Orb
*Wondrous item, rare (requires attunement by a spellcaster)*
:
This milky-white orb swirls with misty images of Thornroot’s menagerie. While holding the orb, you can use an action to cast the *Scrying* spell (no components required) on any enclosure within the menagerie. Once used, the orb goes dark until the next dawn.


#### Thornroot’s Leaf-Shaped Token
*Wondrous item, uncommon*
:
This leaf-shaped token is etched with Thornroot’s signature and glows faintly green when within 30 feet of a creature Thornroot desires for his collection. Presenting the token to Thornroot grants you his favor and hospitality during future visits.

\column

#### Thornroot’s Ledger
*Wondrous item, uncommon (nonmagical)*
:
This thick, leather-bound book contains Thornroot’s notes, sketches, and financial records about his menagerie. The ledger reveals clues about the creatures and dwarves, such as creature habits, the dwarven purchase details, and ward maintenance logs. It also serves as proof that Thornroot bought the dwarves, which may be illegal in some regions.

#### Dwarven Ale (Brinja’s Special Brew)
*Potable item, common*
:
This dark, frothy ale is brewed by Brinja in the dwarves’ hold. It smells strongly of spices and alcohol. Drinking the ale restores 1d4 hit points. Non-dwarves must succeed on a DC 12 Constitution saving throw or be poisoned for 1 hour. Dwarves who drink it crave more and may demand additional servings.
